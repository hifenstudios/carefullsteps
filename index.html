Knowledge

FAQs + Cleaning Tips

Clean sneakers last longer and look better. Here are quick facts and tips you can actually use — simple, safe, and effective.

Use a soft brush + mild soap and cool water. Avoid harsh bleach (it can yellow material over time). Air-dry in shade — direct sun can cause discolouring.
Keep suede as dry as possible. Use a suede brush and spot-clean gently. If it’s soaked or badly stained, don’t scrub hard — that flattens the nap and fades colour.
Light wipe-down weekly, deep clean every 3–6 weeks if you wear them often. The earlier you remove dirt, the easier it is — and the less wear you cause.
Oxidation + UV exposure is the main cause. Store sneakers in a cool, dry place away from sun, and keep them clean. A proper whitening treatment helps when yellowing already happened.
Avoid hot water, strong bleach, and rough scrubbing pads. Heat can warp glues and materials. Harsh chemicals can strip finishes and cause yellowing or cracking.
